      <p class="text-secondary" style="font-family: 'Times New Roman', sans-serif; font-size: 1rem;">
        Conducted a diachronic semiotic analysis of the polyphonic character “Lè/Yuè” (乐), mapping its trajectory across three dimensions. Investigated how the signifier evolved from ritual-political statecraft (Yuè) to secular embodied affect (Lè), and ultimately to cosmological transcendence. Drawing on historical aesthetics and textual analysis, argued this linguistic bifurcation indexes a transformation in Chinese subjectivity: moving from collective normative ordering toward a metaphysics of aesthetic liberation.
